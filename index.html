50 Years in the Making

The material
behind the work.

Fifty years ago, Drytac started with a simple discovery: the material behind the work could be just as important as the work itself.

Richard Kelley was making table mats, coasters, and plaques when a local picture framer asked to buy a roll of the dry mounting tissue he was using. One roll became two — and the numbers spoke for themselves. The future wasn't in table mats. It was in helping others mount, finish, and protect their graphics.

When it came time to build a brand that could stand on its own, the name came straight from the craft: Dry mounting + Tac (tacking it down) = Drytac. Registered the next morning. Still going strong 50 years later.

1976 Founded
Where the Name Came From

A name born from the craft.

Before the name Drytac existed, Richard Kelley ran a small business making table mats, coasters, and wall plaques under the name Lisle Industries — a play on his own name. To him it sounded like a large, impressive manufacturer. In reality, he was a one-person operation.

One day, a local picture framer asked if he could buy a roll of the dry mounting tissue Kelley sourced from a UK supplier. A few days later, the framer ordered again. That quick repeat sale made Kelley realize he'd earned more from two rolls than he had in the previous month selling table mats.

Sitting at his kitchen table late one night, faced with uncertainty about his supplier's future, he decided he needed control. Build his own brand. The name came together naturally:
